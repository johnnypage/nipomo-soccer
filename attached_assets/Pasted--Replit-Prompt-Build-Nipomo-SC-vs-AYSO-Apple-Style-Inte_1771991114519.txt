# Replit Prompt: Build "Nipomo SC vs. AYSO" — Apple-Style Interactive Scroll Page

## Overview

Build a single-page, scroll-driven interactive experience for nipomosc.org that explains the difference between Nipomo SC and AYSO. Think Apple product pages (like the iPhone or AirPods Pro pages) — content that reveals as you scroll, parallax backgrounds, sticky sections, numbers that animate/count up, and text that fades in with purpose.

This is NOT a blog post. It's a story told through scroll. Every section should feel like its own moment.

---

## Brand & Design Direction

- **Primary color:** Dark green (#1a472a)
- **Accent color:** Gold (#c8a951)
- **Background:** Alternate between dark (dark green, near-black #111) and light (warm off-white #fafaf7) sections for dramatic contrast as the user scrolls
- **Typography:** Large, confident serif headings (Georgia, Playfair Display, or similar). Clean sans-serif body. Headings should be BIG — 48px+ on desktop, 32px+ on mobile. Let the words breathe.
- **Imagery:** Use free soccer stock photos (Unsplash/Pexels) as parallax backgrounds. Keywords: youth soccer, kids on field, soccer ball on grass, community sports, soccer goal at sunset. Use dark overlays on images so white/gold text pops.
- **Motion:** Scroll-triggered animations using Framer Motion, GSAP ScrollTrigger, or Intersection Observer. Elements should fade in, slide up, or scale into view as the user reaches them. Numbers should count up when they enter the viewport. Keep it smooth — no jarring transitions.
- **Feel:** Confident, community-driven, premium but approachable. This should feel like a movement, not a memo.

### Section 9: FAQ (Light background, accordion)
Expandable accordion FAQ. Clean, minimal. Click to expand/collapse.

**Is Nipomo SC a non-profit?**
Yes. Nipomo Soccer Club is a registered non-profit organization.

**Will it cost more than AYSO?**
We expect registration to be around $155 — the same as last year's AYSO season. That's despite having to purchase all new equipment from scratch, including goals, balls, and jerseys, and now paying referees.

**Do I have to volunteer?**
Not required per parent, but Nipomo SC depends heavily on community support. Our entire board and all coaches are volunteer. As we grow revenue through fundraisers, sponsorships, and hosting tournaments, we expect to add paid positions over time.

**Can my child play both AYSO and Nipomo SC?**
If your child is in Roots (recreational), yes — they can also play AYSO rec with no conflict. However, if your child plays on a Reign (competitive) team, they cannot also play in Roots or AYSO rec — Cal South does not allow competitive players to be dual-registered in recreational programs. But Reign runs year-round with consistent playing opportunities built in, so there's no gap to fill. Some clubs in the area have relied on AYSO regions to give their competitive players extra playing time. That's not how we operate, and Cal South doesn't allow it.

**Can local businesses sponsor jerseys?**
Yes — this is new. Under AYSO, jersey sponsorships weren't permitted. Email admin@nipomosc.org if interested.

**What ages does Nipomo SC cover?**
Roots (recreational) serves ages 4–14. Visit nipomosc.org for Rise and Reign age ranges.

**When does the season start?**
Visit nipomosc.org for the latest on season dates and registration.

**How can I get involved?**
We're looking for board members, coaches, referees, and volunteers. Email admin@nipomosc.org.

## Technical Requirements

- **Framework:** React (Next.js or Vite) preferred. Plain HTML + GSAP is also fine.
- **Scroll animations:** Use Framer Motion, GSAP ScrollTrigger, or Intersection Observer API. Scroll-triggered fade-ins, slide-ups, and count-up animations on stat numbers.
- **Parallax:** CSS background-attachment: fixed for simple parallax, or GSAP for more controlled parallax effects on the hero, "Same People," and "Why We Left" sections.
- **Charts:** Recharts (if React) or Chart.js. Bars should animate/grow when scrolled into view.
- **Responsive:** Mobile-first. The sticky left/right layout in Section 2 should stack vertically on mobile. Stat cards should be 2x2 grid on mobile. Charts stack on mobile.
- **Performance:** Lazy load images. Use will-change and transform for animations (no layout thrashing). Keep bundle small.
- **Stock images:** Use 3-4 high-quality soccer images from Unsplash. Suggested search terms: "youth soccer field," "soccer ball grass," "kids soccer team," "soccer goal sunset." Apply dark overlays (background: linear-gradient(rgba(0,0,0,0.6), rgba(0,0,0,0.6)), url(...)) for text readability.
- **Smooth scroll:** Implement smooth scrolling between sections.
- **FAQ:** Accordion with smooth expand/collapse animation.
- **Links:** All email addresses should be mailto: links. All nipomosc.org references should link to https://nipomosc.org.

## Design Reference

Look at these Apple pages for the scroll-driven storytelling style:
- https://www.apple.com/airpods-pro/ — sticky sections, text reveals, parallax
- https://www.apple.com/iphone/ — large typography, scroll-triggered animations, alternating dark/light sections

We're not trying to clone Apple. We're borrowing the scroll storytelling pattern and applying it to a community soccer organization. Keep it warm and grounded, not corporate.
